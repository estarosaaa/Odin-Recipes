Sweet and Savory Pork Menudo with Liver

Pork menudo is a classic Filipino dish, a stew featuring pork and often liver, cooked in a tomato-based sauce with potatoes, carrots, and other vegetables like bell peppers and hot dogs.

It's known for its vibrant color and savory-sweet flavor, often incorporating raisins. While traditionally featuring pork and liver, some variations omit the liver for a kid-friendly version.

Ingredients

  • Pork: Usually cubed pork shoulder or butt for a good balance of fat and lean meat
  • Pork Liver: Adds a distinct flavor and richness.
  • Hot dogs
  • Potatoes: Diced potatoes absorb the flavorful sauce.
  • Carrots: Adds sweetness and color.
  • Bell peppers: Both red and green bell peppers are used, diced into chunks.
  • Onionscand Garlic: Aromatics that form the base of the sauce.
  • Peas and raisins: For texture and sweetness.
  • Tomato sauce
  • Soy sauce
  • Bay leaves
  • Salt, pepper, sugar, and sometimes vinegar or calamansi juice for balancing flavor.

How to cook it:

  1. Heat oil in a pan. Once the oil gets hot, start to pan fry the potato and carrots. Continue until all sides turns light brown. Set aside.
  2. Add the pork liver and then stir fry for 30 seconds. Add ground ginger. Continue to stir-fry for 2 minutes. Set aside.

  4. Once the onion becomes soft and translucent, add the pork. Saute until light brown.

  6. Pour soy sauce, tomato sauce, and water into the pot.

  8. Add the pan fried potato and carrots, liver, along with salt and ground black pepper. Stir and cook for 3 minutes.
  9. Transfer to a serving plate then serve.
